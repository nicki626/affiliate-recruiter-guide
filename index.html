Documentation

Affiliate Recruiter Guide

Written for someone sitting at the tool for the first time.

1
What This Tool Does

This tool helps you find and recruit affiliate partners for your program. You bring in a list of potential partners (called prospects), the system pulls real data about them, the AI scores them on how good a fit they are, and then you reach out to the ones worth contacting.

The Five Pipelines

There are five separate pipelines - one for each type of platform:

Website, TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Reddit

Each pipeline is its own tab. They all work similarly but have different data and slightly different steps.

The General Flow

The general flow on every pipeline is:

ADD prospects
›
ENRICH (fetch their real data)
›
SCORE (AI evaluates fit)
›
REVIEW
›
REACH OUT

You do not have to do everything at once. You can import a batch today, enrich tomorrow, and review scores when you have time.

2
Things That Are the Same on Every Page

Before getting into each pipeline, here are things that work the same everywhere.

The Filter Panel

At the top of every pipeline there is a filter panel with four rows. These let you narrow down what you see in the table.

UPDATED row: Filters by when the record was last changed.

"All time"shows everything (default)
"Last hour"useful when enrichment or scoring is running and you want to watch progress
"Today"good starting view for your daily session
"Yesterday"catch up after a day off
"Last 7 days"good for weekly review

STATUS row: Filters by where a prospect is in the process.

"all"shows everything
"pending"added but not yet enriched or scored
"enriched"data has been fetched, ready to score (Website pipeline only)
"scored"AI has scored this prospect
"approved"you manually marked this one as worth pursuing
"rejected"you manually marked this one as not a fit

The number in parentheses next to each status tells you how many records are in that state.

AI SCORE row: Filters by what the AI recommended.

"All"shows everyone with a score
"Strong Yes"score 80-100 (green)
"Yes"score 60-79 (teal/green)
"Maybe"score 40-59 (yellow)
"No"score 0-39 (red)

You can click MULTIPLE options at once. For example, click "Strong Yes" and "Yes" to see only the best prospects.

TAGS row: If you have applied any tags, they appear here as clickable pills. Click a tag to show only prospects with that tag. Click multiple tags to show anyone who has any of those tags.

The Toolbar

Above the table there is a row of buttons. They operate on whatever rows you have selected (checked). Here is what each one does:

SELECT ALL / DESELECT ALL: Checks or unchecks all rows currently visible in the filtered table. It only selects what is visible, so filter first if you want to work on a specific group.

APPROVE (N): Marks selected prospects as "approved." This is just your way of flagging them as worth pursuing. It does not send anything or do anything automatically.

REJECT (N): Marks selected prospects as "rejected." They stay in the system but get out of your way. Use this to keep the pipeline clean.

DELETE (N): Permanently deletes the selected records. There is a confirmation prompt. Cannot be undone - only delete real junk.

TAG INPUT + TAG (N): There is a text box where you type a tag name. Select your prospects, type the tag, click the Tag button. That label is now attached to all selected prospects. You can type a new tag or click the dropdown to pick an existing one. Tags are color-coded and appear under the prospect's name in the table.

DM SENT: On TikTok and Instagram, there is a bulk "DM Sent" button. Select accounts you have messaged, click this, and they get marked as DM Sent. This is a manual tracker - no DMs are actually sent automatically.

The Table

Every pipeline has a table with rows for each prospect. Some columns are the same everywhere:

Checkbox (far left): Click to select this row for bulk actions. Click the checkbox in the header to select all visible rows.

Notes column: If a prospect has notes, you will see the text. If not, you will see small gray text that says "+ note." Click that text once to open an edit box. Type your note. Click somewhere else on the page (or press Ctrl+Enter) and it saves automatically. Use this for anything: email addresses you found manually, impressions from looking at their content, follow-up reminders, anything.

Samples column: A small checkbox. Check it when you have sent this person product samples. That is all it does - it is a manual tracker so you can see at a glance who has received samples.

Updated column: Shows how long ago this record was last changed - "3m ago," "Today," "Yesterday," or a date. Click the column header to sort by most or least recently updated.

Score column: The AI's 0-100 fit score. Click the column header to sort high to low or low to high. A down arrow or up arrow will appear next to "Score" to show which direction you are sorted.

Rec. column: The AI's recommendation as a colored badge: strong_yes (green), yes (lighter green), maybe (yellow), no (red).

Actions column: On the far right of each row there are small buttons. Usually a "Details" button that opens a full detail panel, and a "DM Sent" toggle button for social pipelines.

The Details Panel

Clicking the "Details" button on any row opens a panel with full information about that prospect - all their stats, the AI's reasoning, strengths, weaknesses, and the outreach tool (email or DM generator).

60-79Yes.Solid. Worth reaching out.
40-59Maybe.Use your judgment.
0-39No.Generally skip.
3
Website Pipeline
What This Is For

Recruiting affiliates who have websites - bloggers, review sites, coupon sites, content publishers. You import a list, the system visits each site, reads the content, and the AI scores how good a fit they are.

How to Add Prospects

Upload a CSV export from the publisher recruiting tool you use. Use the import button at the top of the page. The system auto-detects the file format.

Note

If your CSV contains any Instagram or Twitter URLs, those automatically get routed to the Instagram pipeline. You do not need to separate them manually.

Website Columns

Website: The prospect's domain name. Click it to open their website in a new tab. If you see a URL that looks wrong or broken, that came from the CSV.

Niche: This shows up after enrichment. It's a small badge showing the content style (like "Blog" or "Review Site") plus topic tags the scraper found (like "health," "wellness," "pharmacy"). If this column is empty, the site has not been enriched yet.

Aff. Signals: Also shows up after enrichment. These badges tell you whether the prospect is actively doing affiliate marketing and which networks they use. Here is what each signal means:

Green Badges (strongest signals):

✓ Disclosure - We found an affiliate disclosure page on the site (e.g. /affiliate-disclosure, /disclaimer). The FTC requires affiliates to have this page. If they have one, they are actively doing affiliate marketing. This is the single most reliable signal.

awin, shareasale, cj affiliate, rakuten, impact radius, clickbank, skimlinks - We found a specific affiliate network name mentioned on the page. They are actively using that network. "awin" is especially useful because ClearWellness360 is on Awin - they can sign up immediately.

Yellow Badge (moderate signal):

"4 signals" (number varies) - We found affiliate-related keywords in the page content like "affiliate link," "commission," "use code," "#sponsored," "brand ambassador," or "this post contains." No specific network was detected, but they are likely doing affiliate work.

Red Text (weak or no signal):

"none found" - No affiliate signals or disclosure page detected. This does not necessarily mean they are not an affiliate - they might use cloaked links that are hard to detect. Treat as cold outreach, lower priority.

"scrape failed" - Could not load the page at all. Unknown status.

Outreach Priority Order:

1. ✓ Disclosure + awin - Perfect match. Already on CW360's network.

2. ✓ Disclosure + any network - Active affiliate, knows the process.

3. ✓ Disclosure alone - Confirmed affiliate, just need to pitch.

4. Network name alone - Active affiliate, no disclosure page found.

5. Keyword signals only - Likely affiliate, worth reaching out.

6. None found - Cold outreach, lower priority.

Score / Rec: The AI's score and recommendation. Empty until you run Analyze.

Updated: When this record was last changed.

Notes / Samples: See section 2 above.

Website Toolbar Buttons

The extra ones specific to this pipeline:

ENRICH PENDING: This button visits every prospect's website and scrapes real content. It looks for affiliate network mentions, contact email addresses, what topics they cover, and what type of content they publish. This takes time - it is running a real web browser on each site. The button turns GREEN when there are pending (unenriched) rows waiting. That is your signal to click it. After enrichment, you will see the Niche and Aff. Signals columns fill in.

ANALYZE ENRICHED: After enrichment, click this to send all enriched data to the AI for scoring. The button turns GREEN when there are enriched rows ready to score. Run Enrich first, then Analyze. The system will not score a prospect that has not been enriched.

DELETE SELECTED: Permanently deletes the selected rows.

The Details Panel (Website)

Click "Details" on any row to open the full panel. It has two sections:

ENRICH RESULTS section: Shows you what the scraper actually found: topics, affiliate networks, a contact email if one was on the page, and the content style. This is what the AI used as input.

AI ANALYSIS section: Shows the AI's score, its written reasoning (2-3 sentences), a list of strengths, and a list of weaknesses. If you see "No analysis yet," this prospect has not been scored.

Generate Email button: At the bottom of the panel. Click it to generate a personalized outreach email for this prospect. The email uses their site content and your program details. After generating, you can edit the text directly and then click "Send via Outlook" to send it.

How Website Scoring Works

The AI checks three gates in order. Failing any gate caps the score immediately and nothing after that matters.

Gate 1 - Red Flags

If the site matches anything on your red flags list (configured in Settings), the score is capped at 20 and it is always a No. End of evaluation.

Gate 2 - Affiliate Presence

We only want people who are already running affiliate traffic. If they have never done it, we move on.

Zero networks, zero signals, not running Facebook Ads = score capped at 25. Always No.

1-2 signals only, no Facebook Ads = score capped at 50. Capped at Maybe.

3+ partners, OR a recognized network found, OR 2+ signals, OR running Facebook Ads = affiliate gate cleared, move to Gate 3.

Special note: Running Facebook Ads counts as proof they understand promotion and traffic. It automatically clears the affiliate gate even if their affiliate network presence is thin.

Gate 3 - Niche Fit

No connection to your target niches = score capped at 35. Always No.

Adjacent or tangential niche = score capped at 55. Capped at Maybe.

Strong niche match = proceed to full scoring.

FINAL SCORING (only if all three gates cleared)

80-100Strong YesStrong niche + 5 or more affiliate partners + bonus signals present.
60-79YesGood niche + 3 or more affiliate partners + content type aligns.
40-59MaybeRight niche + only 1-2 partners, OR strong affiliate history but weaker niche.
0-39NoFailed a gate.

WHAT PUSHES A SCORE UP WITHIN A BAND

Being on a preferred affiliate network, high relevance score from your research tool, review or comparison content style, content that directly speaks to your audience's pain points. These do not change which band you land in - they move you up within the band.

TikTok Pipeline
What This Is For

Recruiting TikTok creators - health, wellness, fitness, pharmacy, nutrition niches. This pipeline is different from Website in one important way: when you import a file and click the Import button, the system automatically enriches AND scores the accounts in one go. You do not run separate Enrich and Analyze steps.

How to Add TikTok Accounts

OPTION 1: IMPORT FROM CSV

Upload a CSV or TSV file exported from your creator research tool.

The system reads the file, pulls out all TikTok handles, shows you a preview: "Found 84 unique TikTok handles. Sample: @handle1, @handle2..."

It also shows match rate counts - how many were rated Excellent, Good, or Average by your research tool.

Before clicking Import, fill in the "Source tag" field. This automatically tags every imported account with a label like "Research Tool > TikTok > Wellness" so you know where it came from later.

Then click "Import & Auto-Score."

A progress bar appears showing three steps: Step 1/3 Importing, Step 2/3 Enriching (X/Y accounts), Step 3/3 Scoring (X/Y).

When it finishes, you get a toast message: "Import complete - 84 accounts imported, 3 duplicates skipped, 81 scored."

OPTION 2: DISCOVER BY KEYWORD

Below the import panel there is a "Discover by Keyword" section.

Enter keywords separated by commas (example: "cold sore remedies, lip care, herpes treatment").

Set Min Followers and Max Followers to filter by account size.

Set Results per Keyword (default 50).

Click Discover. A progress bar appears while it searches TikTok.

When done, the accounts appear in the table below already enriched and ready to score.

After discovery you still need to click "Score All Pending" in the toolbar to get AI scores.

What the Gold Star and Red X Mean

In the second column of the table, every row has either a gold star (looks like ✦) or a red X.

Gold star = this account was successfully enriched. The system fetched real data from TikTok - followers, videos, likes, bio, location. This account is ready to score.

Red X = enrichment failed or has not run yet. The follower count will show 0. You can re-enrich these by selecting them and clicking "Re-enrich."

TikTok Columns

Handle: The TikTok username as a blue clickable link - click to open their profile. Their display name appears below the handle in smaller text. Any tags you have applied appear as colored pills under the name.

Followers: Their total follower count. Shown in shorthand: 45.2K, 1.3M, etc. Under 5,000 = automatic No by hard rule, no matter what else is true. Sweet spot: 10K to 2M.

Avg Views: Average views per video followed by the view rate in parentheses - example: "82.4K (18.2%)." The percentage is average views divided by followers. This is the key engagement metric on TikTok. Over 20% = excellent. 10-20% = good. 5-10% = acceptable. Under 5% = weak. Can exceed 100% if their content regularly goes viral on the For You page.

Videos: Total number of videos they have posted. More = more active and consistent creator.

Score: The AI's 0-100 fit score. Click the column header to sort. Arrow shows sort direction.

Rec: The AI recommendation badge: strong_yes (green), yes (lighter green), maybe (yellow), no (red).

Aff: Whether the AI found affiliate signals. A green checkmark means yes - link in bio, discount code, #ad, TikTok Shop, brand partnership language, etc. A dash means no signals were found. Important: no affiliate signals = score capped at 50 no matter what else is true.

Match: If the account was imported from your creator research tool, this shows the tool's own match rating: Excellent, Good, or Average. Color coded. If blank, the account was added another way.

Bio: A preview of their TikTok bio, truncated to 80 characters. See the full bio in the Details panel.

Status: A badge showing where this account is in the process: pending, scored, approved, rejected. If an email has been generated for this account, you will see a small blue "email ready" note under the status badge. If the account has been contacted via DM, you will see a green "DM sent" note.

Updated: How long ago this record last changed. Click the column header to sort.

Notes / Samples: See section 2 above.

Actions: Two small buttons at the end of each row: "Details" - opens the full detail panel for this account. "DM Sent" toggle button - click to mark as DM sent, click again to unmark. Turns green with a checkmark when marked.

TikTok Toolbar Buttons

SCORE / SCORE ALL PENDING: Sends the enriched data to the AI for scoring. If you have accounts selected, it scores those. If nothing is selected, it scores all pending accounts. The button turns GREEN when there are unscored accounts waiting. A progress bar appears while scoring runs.

RE-ENRICH (N): Select accounts with red X marks or zero follower counts. Click this to re-run enrichment on those specific accounts. Shows "(N)" where N is the number selected.

APPROVE (N) / REJECT (N) / DELETE (N): Standard bulk actions. See section 2.

DM SENT (N): Marks all selected accounts as DM Sent.

TAG INPUT + TAG (N): There is a text box where you type a tag name. Select your prospects, type the tag, click the Tag button.

GENERATE EMAILS (Yes+): Generates outreach emails for all accounts scored Yes or Strong Yes. If you have accounts selected, it generates only for those. If nothing is selected, it generates for all Yes and Strong Yes accounts. A counter appears while generating. When done, "email ready" appears in the Status column for each account.

REGENERATE ALL (N): Visible only after emails exist. Click to regenerate all previously generated emails - useful if you updated your program details and want fresh emails.

EXPORT CSV: Downloads the current table view as a CSV file. If accounts are selected, exports only those. Includes handle, followers, avg views, score, recommendation, affiliate signals, contact email, bio, and AI reasoning.

SEND VIA OUTLOOK (N): Sends generated emails for the selected accounts via your connected Outlook account. Only sends to accounts that already have a generated email AND are selected.

Important

Send in batches of 10-15 at a time. Do not send to everyone at once. Large send bursts trigger spam filters.

The Details Panel (TikTok)

Click "Details" on any row to open the full panel.

At the top: a link to their TikTok profile.

Stats grid: Followers, Avg Views, View Rate, Videos in large bold numbers. Below that: Total Likes and Location.

Research Tool Match: If imported from your creator research tool, shows the match rating and a sample post title.

Contact Email field: If an email was found in their TikTok bio during enrichment, it pre-fills here. You can edit it manually and click Save. This is the email address the outreach goes to.

Bio: Their full TikTok bio text.

AI Score section (if scored): Large fit score number, recommendation badge, the AI's written reasoning, a bulleted list of strengths, and a bulleted list of weaknesses. The weaknesses section is especially useful - if a hard rule failed, it will say so here (e.g. "No affiliate signals detected - score capped at 50").

Outreach Email section: At the bottom. If no email has been generated yet, you will see a "Generate Email" button. Click it. If an email already exists, you will see the subject line and full email body in a box. There is also a "Regenerate" button if you want a fresh version. The "To:" line shows where it will be sent. Click "Send via Outlook" to send directly from this panel for just this one account.

How TikTok Scoring Works

Three hard rules are checked first. Any one of them immediately caps the score.

Hard Rule 1 - Red Flag Match

If the handle or bio matches anything on your red flags list, score is capped at 20. Always No. The weaknesses section in the Details panel will tell you which flag triggered.

Hard Rule 2 - Under 5,000 Followers

Score is capped at 35. Always No. Too small to generate meaningful affiliate revenue.

Hard Rule 3 - No Affiliate Signals

Score is capped at 50. Always Maybe. The AI found no evidence they run affiliate content - no link in bio, no discount codes, no #ad, no TikTok Shop, no brand mentions. They might still be worth a reach, but do not expect high conversion.

If all three hard rules are cleared, the AI evaluates:

NICHE FIT (most important factor): The AI reads handle name, bio keywords, and content signals together. A fitness coach, naturopath, or pharmacist bio = strong match. This is the biggest factor.

VIEW RATE (engagement): Over 20% = excellent. 10-20% = good. 5-10% = acceptable. Under 5% = weak (flagged in weaknesses but not an automatic disqualifier on its own).

FINAL BANDS

80-100Strong YesStrong niche + view rate 10%+ + clear affiliate signals.
60-79YesGood niche + view rate 5%+ + at least one affiliate signal.
40-59MaybeRight niche + some engagement but no affiliate signals, OR good signals but softer niche.
0-39NoWrong niche, red flag, under 5k, or no signals plus weak engagement.

BONUS FACTORS (push score up within a band)

US location is preferred. Non-US is flagged as a weakness. Verified badge is a mild positive. High cumulative total likes shows historically engaging content.

Instagram Pipeline
What This Is For

Recruiting Instagram creators in health, wellness, beauty, and fitness niches. This pipeline is different from Website in one important way: when you import a CSV in the Website pipeline, any Instagram handles in that file are automatically sent here. You do not add them manually.

How to Add Instagram Accounts

OPTION 1: Automatic from CSV Import: When you upload a CSV on the Website pipeline, the system reads every URL. Any TikTok or Instagram URLs get routed here automatically. You will see them appear as pending accounts.

OPTION 2: Lookalike Seeds: At the top of the Instagram pipeline there is a "Lookalike Seeds" section. Enter the handle of a creator you already like and the system will search for similar accounts. Use your best existing prospects as seeds to find more like them.

Instagram Columns

Handle: Their Instagram username as a clickable link. Tags appear under the name.

Followers: Total follower count. Sweet spot: 5,000 to 500,000.

Avg Likes: Average likes per post. Higher relative to follower count = more engaged audience.

Engagement %: Average likes divided by followers. This is the most important metric on Instagram. Under 1% on a non-private account = automatic No. 1-2% = acceptable. 2-5% = good. Over 5% = excellent.

Aff: Green checkmark or dash. Same as TikTok - checkmark means affiliate signals found (link in bio, discount codes, #ad, brand partnerships). Dash means none found. No affiliate signals = score capped at 50.

DM Sent: A small indicator in the Status column. Green "DM sent" appears when marked.

Score / Rec / Updated / Notes / Samples: Same as everywhere else.

Instagram Toolbar Buttons

ENRICH PENDING: Fetches live Instagram data for accounts that have not been enriched yet. The button turns GREEN when there are accounts with 0 followers (meaning not yet enriched). Click it to pull real follower counts, engagement rates, bios, and hashtags.

SCORE PENDING: Sends enriched accounts to the AI for scoring. Run Enrich first.

DM SENT (N): Marks all selected accounts as DM Sent.

Other buttons are the same as TikTok.

How Instagram Scoring Works

Mostly the same as TikTok. Key difference: engagement rate is measured by likes rather than views, so the percentage thresholds are lower.

Under 1% engagement = automatic No.

1-2% = acceptable.

2-5% = good.

Over 5% = excellent.

Same hard rules apply: under 5k followers, no affiliate signals, red flag match.

YouTube Pipeline
What This Is For

Recruiting YouTube creators. More evergreen content than TikTok - videos stay discoverable for years. Works best for creators who make tutorials, reviews, or how-to content.

YouTube Columns

Channel: The channel name as a clickable link.

Subscribers: Total subscriber count.

Avg Views: Average views per video. On YouTube, view rate (views divided by subscribers) matters more than raw views. A smaller channel with 50% view rate often outperforms a bigger channel with 5% view rate.

Videos: Total videos on the channel.

Other columns same as other pipelines.

How YouTube Scoring Works

Same three hard rules apply. Key differences in final scoring:

VIEW RATE: Over 30% = excellent (YouTube-specific threshold). 10-30% = good. Under 10% = flagged as weakness.

CONTENT TYPE: Tutorials, reviews, how-tos, and comparison videos are scored highest because they convert to affiliate clicks best. Vlogs and entertainment content score lower because viewers are not in buying mode.

Reddit Pipeline
What This Is For

Recruiting subreddit moderators as affiliates. This is a different model from the other pipelines - you are not looking at individual creators, you are looking at communities and their moderators.

Reddit Columns

Subreddit: The community name (like r/SkincareAddiction). Clickable link.

Members: Total subscriber count for that subreddit.

Moderators: List of moderator usernames. These are your targets.

Activity: Posts per day. Higher = more engaged community.

How Reddit Scoring Works

Different from other pipelines. The AI scores the community, not the individual.

NICHE FIT: How closely does the subreddit topic align with your product?

SIZE: Bigger is not always better. Communities with 10K-500K members often have more engaged audiences than massive subreddits.

ACTIVITY: A dead subreddit is worthless. The AI checks posts per day and comment ratios.

MODERATOR ACCESSIBILITY: Some moderator teams are known to be open to partnerships. Others are hostile. The AI checks for signals of either.

Outreach - Sending Emails and DMs
Email Outreach

Step 1 - Make sure there is an email address: The system tries to find emails during enrichment. If it found one, you will see it in the Details panel. If not, you can manually add one in the "Contact Email" field.

Step 2 - Generate the email: Click "Generate Email" in the Details panel, OR use the bulk "Generate Emails (Yes+)" button in the toolbar to generate emails for all Yes and Strong Yes prospects at once. The AI writes a personalized email using your program details and the prospect's profile.

Step 3 - Review and edit: The generated email appears in the Details panel. Read it. Edit if needed - just click in the text and type.

Step 4 - Send: Click "Send via Outlook" to send from your connected Outlook account. Or use the bulk "Send via Outlook" button in the toolbar to send to multiple selected prospects at once.

Important

Send in batches of 10-15 at a time. Do not send to everyone at once. Large send bursts trigger spam filters and can get your domain blacklisted.

DM Outreach (TikTok / Instagram)

DMs are not sent automatically. This is a manual process with a tracker.

Step 1: Open the prospect's profile by clicking their handle.

Step 2: Send them a DM manually through TikTok or Instagram.

Step 3: Come back to the tool and click the "DM Sent" button on their row, or select multiple rows and click the bulk "DM Sent" button.

This marks them as contacted so you do not accidentally message them again.

In the Details panel, there is also a "Generate DM" button that writes a short DM script for you. Copy that text and paste it into the actual DM on TikTok or Instagram.

Settings

The Settings page configures how the tool works for your program. Access it from the gear icon in the top right.

Program URL

The URL where affiliates sign up for your program. This gets included in outreach emails. Example: https://yourprogram.partnerize.com/signup

Outlook Connection

Click "Connect Outlook" to link your Microsoft account. This is required for the "Send via Outlook" feature to work. You will see a green checkmark when connected.

Tags Catalog

Pre-define common tags here so they appear in the dropdown when tagging prospects. You can also assign colors to each tag. Tags created on-the-fly in the table are added here automatically.

Program Details

This is the most important section. The AI uses everything here when scoring prospects and writing emails. Fill this in completely.

Brand Name: Your company or product name.

Product Description: What you sell, in 1-2 sentences.

Target Niches: The topics and audiences you want affiliates to reach. Be specific. "Health" is too broad. "Cold sore treatment, lip care, oral health, pharmacy, dermatology" is better.

Commission Structure: What affiliates earn. Example: "15% on first purchase, 10% recurring."

Red Flags: Words, phrases, or patterns that disqualify a prospect automatically. Examples: competitor brand names, controversial topics, unrelated niches. Any prospect matching a red flag gets an automatic No with a capped score.

Preferred Networks: Affiliate networks you already work with. Prospects already on these networks score slightly higher.

Email Tone: How formal or casual your outreach should be. Options: Professional, Friendly, Casual.

Email Signature: What goes at the bottom of every email.

Example configuration:

Brand Name: Abreva Product Description: FDA-approved cold sore treatment that heals cold sores fast. Target Niches: cold sore treatment, lip care, oral health, pharmacy, dermatology, skincare, health and wellness Commission Structure: 15% on first purchase, 10% on repeat purchases Red Flags: competitor (Carmex, Blistex), MLM, crypto, gambling, adult content, political Preferred Networks: ShareASale, CJ, Impact, Partnerize Email Tone: Friendly Email Signature: Best, [Your Name] Abreva Affiliate Team
